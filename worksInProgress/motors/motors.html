Motors

Anything that moves has potential for making sound, and motors, by definition, move. Usually the movement is the part we want to exploit, but artists have been exploring the sonic potential of motors (e.g. the work of Zimoun or this example of stepper motor music)

Motors is a musical instrument consisting of many different motors and motor mechanisms. The motors are not embellished with any additional sound making devices, so that the sound heard is only that of the motor and the mechanism. Motors and mechanisms are preferably salvaged or found, but new motors and mechanisms are used as well. Each device is individually controlled via MIDI.

Motors is not a stand-alone structure; rather, it is a site-specific installation mounted along walls, hanging from the ceiling, and on the floor. Some free standing structures may be used as well.

Like other musical instruments, the output of this instrument is the music it produces. I collaborate with composers who would compose a piece specifically for the site. My collaborators also advise during the instrument construction to achieve the desired sounds.

Composition can begin only when the instrument is complete, as until then we can't be sure what it will sound like. The composer controls each device via a MIDI keyboard, and by listening to the resultant sounds decides what to compose. The resultant composition can be pre-programmed or performed live, and can potentially be accompanied by other instruments.
